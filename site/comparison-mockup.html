2AI + Portland data access
Claude · MCP tools
What this resident walks away with "OK, my lot is R2.5, ADU is allowed up to 800 sf, and I need to check Title 33.205 — but what's an SDC and which permit do I file first?"

For 4521 SE Belmont St: base zone is R2.5 (single-dwelling); no overlay zones detected. ADU permitted under Title 33.205.

Per PCC 33.205.040: max ADU size is 800 sf or 75% of main dwelling, whichever is less. Setbacks per 33.110.220: front 10', side 5', rear 5'. Height limit 20'.

Permits: building, plumbing, electrical, mechanical. SDCs apply — water, sewer, transportation, parks. Stormwater management may be required if impervious area threshold is exceeded.

Claude · MCP + enrichment
What this resident walks away with "I know my zone, my limits, my next three calls, and which permits to file in what order. I can act on Monday morning."

Your property: 4521 SE Belmont, zoned R2.5. ADU is allowed. A "tiny house on wheels" is not permitted as a permanent residence — you need a tiny house on foundation, treated as an ADU.

Size and placement (PCC 33.205.040): up to 800 sf, max 20' tall. Setbacks (PCC 33.110.220): 10' front, 5' sides, 5' rear. Rear yard placement is easiest.

Costs to budget: System Development Charges (SDCs) for water, sewer, transportation, parks — typically $15,000–$25,000. Building permit fees separate.

Your next steps, in order: (1) Free zoning confirmation at BDS, (2) site plan, (3) building permit application, (4) plumbing/electrical/mechanical sub-permits, (5) erosion control before excavation.

How to read the highlights

  • good Specific, verifiable, correct citation
  • meh Vague or unverified — directionally right
  • bad Wrong number or contradicted by code
  • missing AI admitted (or should have admitted) it can't answer

The pattern across all 7 scenarios

  • Out-of-the-box: sounds confident, often wrong on numbers, citations are vague.
  • + Data access (MCP): citations become specific and accurate. Language gets technical.
  • + Better question (enrichment): answer becomes actionable plain-English guidance the resident can use.
